Audit Methodology

Security audits focused on real-world risks, attacker behavior and practical impact — not compliance checklists.

Audit Philosophy

Risk Over Compliance

The goal is to reduce actual risk, not to satisfy a checklist or generate a false sense of security.

Attacker Mindset

CI/CD pipelines are treated as an attack surface, not as trusted internal automation.

Context Matters

Findings are evaluated in the context of your workflows, team practices and threat model.

Threat Modeling

Pipeline Entry Points

Pull requests, forks, scheduled jobs, manual triggers and third-party integrations.

Privilege & Secrets

Token scope, secret propagation, environment exposure and escalation paths.

Artifact Trust

Build artifacts, caches, dependencies and untrusted inputs moving through the pipeline.

Automation and Manual Analysis

Automated Signals

Static analysis, secret detection and configuration checks provide initial signals, not final answers.

Human Judgment

Manual review identifies logic flaws, abuse cases and risks that scanners cannot detect.

Risk Prioritization

Findings are prioritized based on impact, likelihood and exploitability.

Reporting Principles

Clear Severity Levels

Each issue is classified with transparent severity and rationale.

Actionable Guidance

Recommendations focus on practical fixes, not abstract best practices.

Technical & Executive Views

Reports are structured for both engineers and decision-makers.

Security Is a Process

An audit is a point-in-time assessment designed to improve long-term security posture, not a one-time checkbox.
