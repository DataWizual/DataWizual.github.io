Vulnerability Disclosure Policy (VDP)

Version 1.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2026

This policy governs ongoing, good-faith security research conducted by DataWizual. The purpose is to identify security risks in publicly accessible software artifacts and to coordinate responsible disclosure in a professional, non-intrusive manner.

DataWizual focuses on static analysis, architectural review, and supply-chain risk assessment of modern CI/CD workflows and infrastructure code. No exploitation or active intrusion is performed.

1. Scope of Research

In Scope
  • Public Git repositories
  • CI/CD workflow definitions
  • Infrastructure-as-Code templates
  • Build and deployment configuration files
Out of Scope
  • Private or proprietary systems
  • Live service exploitation or runtime attacks
  • Denial-of-Service testing
  • Social engineering or phishing

2. Research Principles

  • Non-Intrusive: Analysis is limited to publicly available code and configuration.
  • No Exploitation: Vulnerabilities are never actively exploited.
  • Privacy-First: No access to private data, systems, or credentials.
  • Automation with Care: Automated static analysis tools are used only on public artifacts.

3. Reporting & Coordination Process

When a potential security risk is identified, DataWizual follows a coordinated disclosure process:

  1. Initial private notification via official security contact channels.
  2. Provision of a high-level description of the risk and remediation guidance.
  3. No public disclosure prior to remediation or mutual agreement.

DataWizual will acknowledge receipt of reports and communications within 3–7 business days when acting as the receiving party.

4. Disclosure Timeline

Findings remain confidential until a fix is available or a standard 90-day coordination window has passed. This timeline may be extended by mutual agreement between parties.

5. Safe Harbor

This policy is intended to provide Safe Harbor for security research conducted in good faith and in accordance with this document. DataWizual does not engage in extortion, public shaming, or coercive disclosure practices.

All activities are performed solely to improve the security and resilience of the software supply chain.
