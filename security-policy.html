Security Disclosure Policy

Last Updated: January 1, 2026

At DataWizual, we believe that the security of the open-source ecosystem is a shared responsibility. This policy outlines our commitment to responsible security research and coordinated disclosure.

1. Responsible Disclosure Principles

We perform non-intrusive security audits of public repositories to validate our internal security engines. Our research is guided by the following rules:

  • Non-Intrusive: We only analyze publicly available source code, configurations, and IaC templates.
  • No Exploitation: We do not attempt to exploit vulnerabilities, bypass authentication, or access private data.
  • Confidentiality: Findings are reported privately to the project owners. We do not publish details until a fix is available or a 90-day coordination window has passed.

2. Reporting Process

If we identify a potential risk, we reach out via official security channels (e.g., security@company.com or Bug Bounty platforms). Our reports include a detailed description of the risk pattern and recommended remediation steps.

3. Safe Harbor

We expect project owners to provide a "Safe Harbor" for researchers who act in good faith. We do not support or engage in extortion or public shaming. Our goal is to make the software supply chain safer.
