Vulnerability Disclosure Policy (VDP)

Version 1.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2026

This policy governs ongoing security research conducted by DataWizual. We are committed to protecting the software ecosystem through ethical, non-intrusive analysis and coordinated vulnerability disclosure.

1. Scope of Research

✔ In-Scope
  • • Public source code repositories
  • • Public Infrastructure-as-Code (IaC)
  • • Public CI/CD configuration files
  • • Published Docker images / Artifacts
✘ Out-of-Scope
  • • Production SaaS / Live environments
  • • User data & Private databases
  • • Denial of Service (DoS) attacks
  • • Social Engineering / Phishing

2. Terms of Good Faith (Safe Harbor)

DataWizual conducts automated static analysis on public artifacts only. We maintain Safe Harbor by adhering to the following:

  • No Intent to Harm: Our goal is defensive. We do not destroy data or disrupt services.
  • No Exploitation: We do not bypass authentication or interact with runtime systems.
  • No Circumvention: We do not attempt to bypass technical security controls of the platforms hosting the code.

3. Coordinated Disclosure & Timeline

We follow a 90-day disclosure window. This timeline is flexible and may be extended upon mutual agreement based on the complexity of the fix.

  • Reports are sent privately to the dedicated security contact.
  • Public disclosure is only performed under coordinated conditions and after a fix is verified.
  • We do not seek financial compensation (extortion) for disclosing findings.
